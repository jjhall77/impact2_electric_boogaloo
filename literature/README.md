# Literature

Organized reading list for the replication and extension paper.

## Core paper
- MacDonald, Fagan & Geller (2016). The Effects of Local Police Surges
  on Crime and Arrests in New York City. *PLOS ONE* 11(6): e0157223.

## Methodological (staggered DD)
- Callaway & Sant'Anna (2021). Difference-in-differences with multiple
  time periods. *J. Econometrics* 225(2): 200–230.
- Sun & Abraham (2021). Estimating dynamic treatment effects in event
  studies with heterogeneous treatment effects. *J. Econometrics* 225(2).
- de Chaisemartin & D'Haultfœuille (2020). Two-way fixed effects
  estimators with heterogeneous treatment effects. *AER* 110(9).
- Goodman-Bacon (2021). Difference-in-differences with variation in
  treatment timing. *J. Econometrics* 225(2).

## Operation Impact / SQF
- Weisburd et al. (2015). Do Stop, Question, and Frisk Practices Deter
  Crime? *Criminology & Public Policy*.
- Fagan (2016). Terry's original sin. *U. Chicago Legal Forum*.
- Goel, Rao & Shroff (2016). Precinct or prejudice? *Annals of Applied
  Statistics* 10(1).
- Golden & Almo (2004). Reducing gun violence: An overview of NYC's
  strategies. Vera Institute.
